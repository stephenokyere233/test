Reclaim Your Creative Confidence

Creativity is not a question of talent; it's a mindset. There is no “creative type”; we are all unique and inventive in our own ways. But in order to achieve peak creativity, we must first confront our inner critics and develop a beginner's mentality. Creativity isn't easy, but with these tips, you can conquer your doubts and start creating today!

Creativity is not a question of talent; it’s a mindset.

Creativity is not a question of talent, it's a mindset. Creative confidence is a way of thinking and approaching problems. It’s not just about being able to make things, but being able to look at the world in unconventional ways and asking questions that no one has asked before. It’s all about trying new things and maybe failing, but learning from those mistakes until you succeed—which will happen eventually if you’re persistent enough (and you should be). Being creative means doing research on what other people have done before, so that you know where your ideas fit into the bigger picture (or don't fit).

The only way to get creative is to do creative work.

Creativity is a skill, and like any skill it can be learned. Creative confidence is not a talent. It's not about being born with the creative gene, but rather about developing your creative muscles and becoming more comfortable using them on a regular basis. Creativity isn't just about making things, but also learning new things—it's an iterative process of experimentation, failure and growth.

Know that there’s no such thing as a “creative type”.

Don’t limit yourself to a particular type of creative work. Don’t be afraid to try new things, even if they don’t feel like the “right fit”. And don’t be afraid to fail; it just means you have more experience under your belt!

Don’t let your inner critics stop you from creating.

  • Don’t let your inner critic stop you from trying.
  • Don’t let your inner critic stop you from learning.
  • Don’t let your inner critic stop you from doing.
  • Don’t let your inner critic stop you from being and connecting with other people in the world who might be able to help you become the best version of yourself that you can be!

Make your mark.

A desire to make a mark on the world is one of the most common reasons people think about leaving their jobs. They want to do something that matters, be known for something and feel proud of themselves in the present tense.

Embrace the “beginner’s mindset”.

Be open to new ideas and ways of thinking. Be willing to try new things. Be willing to learn from your mistakes, even if it means that you have to do something differently than you’re used to doing it or with a different process than the one that has worked for you in the past. Ask others for help and feedback—and accept their honest responses without judgement or ego-based defensiveness, regardless of what they might say about how your work looks or sounds. Take risks on projects that inspire you—even if they feel risky at first! Consider what happens when we don’t take risks: life becomes boring, stagnant...and unfulfilling as well as uninspiring if we stay inside our comfort zones forever (which I promise will happen). If we never take a risk in life then why bother being alive at all? What fun is that?! It’s better than dying young though so please do try not too die!

Expose yourself to new experiences, new ideas, and new people in order to spark inspiration.

Once you've created a safe space for yourself to play and explore, the next step is to expose yourself to new experiences. As I mentioned earlier, creativity thrives on inspiration. The more you expose yourself to new things (and people), the more likely you'll be able to find inspiration in them. The world is filled with amazing things—you just have to be willing and open enough to see them all. Be open-minded; try something new; be brave! Think about how much fun it is when we allow ourselves time and space for creativity in our lives by going on vacation or going out with friends on weekends. Why not bring that same energy into our everyday lives? If we want our creative confidence back, we need to start looking at all aspects of life as opportunities for creative expression instead of restrictions on what we can do or think about doing creatively: exposing ourselves daily gives us access every day rather than waiting until something "exciting" happens before taking action."

You may have creative confidence and not even know it!

You may already have creative confidence, and don’t even know it! Creative confidence is a mindset. It’s not about talent or natural ability—it’s about doing creative work and learning from your mistakes. Creative confidence comes from research, trying new things, making mistakes (and loving them), getting feedback, and finding your own style.
