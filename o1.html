We all bleed the same color

Last week, I was walking past a school and heard a kid say to his friend: "I don't understand how that is. We all bleed the same color. Why do people hate each other?" Simple words from a child, but it's amazing how few adults recognize this basic fact of life. We are all born with blood that is the same color, so why do we use it to hurt others? What if we used it to help one another instead?

It's a simple fact, but it's amazing how many people don't recognize it.

It's a simple fact, but it's amazing how many people don't recognize it. Our blood all has the same color—in fact, it's called "red" because of this. Every single one of us bleeds red when we get cut or injured, and our hearts pump red when they're beating. This means that the person in front of you at Starbucks has the same blood as you do; if someone were to slit his throat right now and bleed out on the floor while screaming in pain, his face would turn bright red just like yours would if that happened to you. The thing about blood is that we all have so much of it; it makes sense for us not to think about what happens when some gets spilled out onto our hands or clothes or into a cup at Starbucks (which is why this article exists). When I was younger I used to think about how horrifying it would be if somebody spilled their drink inside my backpack; now I know better than anyone else does how wrong my original perspective was!

We all bleed the same color.

We all bleed red. The color of our blood is not a marker of who we are, or what we’ve done. We are all the same; humans with red blood pumping through our veins. To think otherwise is to deny your own humanity and that of others—which is exactly what those who try to pit us against each other have been trying to do for centuries, in order to justify their own hatefulness and greed for power. We don't have different colored blood; it's just that some people let it show more than others.

Remember the blood in your body is the same as the blood in your neighbor or a stranger

All blood is red. All blood is the same red. All blood has the same color, the same makeup, and carries oxygen to all parts of your body that need it. As a human being, you have an obligation to keep your body healthy by making sure your blood stays clean and free-flowing—make an appointment with your doctor if you need help with that!

Humans are not perfect, and neither is this world we live in, but it's important to remember that differences aside — whether it be race, religion or even political views — people are all the same. What makes us unique as individuals is what brings us together as a society. We should celebrate our differences and influence each other with our opposing views because without diversity, the world would be one dull place!
